BMW M5 E60 – The Legendary V10 Sports Sedan (2005–2010)

The BMW M5 E60 is an iconic sports sedan produced between 2005 and 2010. A true standout in the automotive world, it’s powered by a 5.0-liter naturally aspirated V10 (engine code S85) producing 507 hp and 520 Nm of torque — the first time such an engine was used in a production sedan.

Most models came with a 7-speed SMG III automated manual transmission, while a 6-speed manual was available in certain markets.

Key Specifications

  • Engine: 5.0-liter naturally aspirated V10 (S85), 507 hp, 520 Nm.
  • Transmission: Rear-wheel drive, 7-speed SMG III or 6-speed manual.
  • Performance: 0–100 km/h in 4.7 s, top speed 250 km/h (electronically limited) or 305 km/h with the M Driver’s Package.
  • Chassis: Adaptive suspension, limited-slip differential, variable-ratio steering.
  • Design: Refined BMW interior with M-specific touches, unique wheels, large brakes, and aggressive air intakes.
  • Body Styles: Sedan and Touring (E61).

Strengths

  • Iconic V10 Engine: Unique in its class, delivering unmatched sound and driving feel.
  • Performance: Rapid acceleration and sharp handling thanks to a precise chassis.
  • Design: A perfect blend of elegance and sportiness with unmistakable M styling cues.

Things to Watch Out For

  • Reliability: Rod bearing wear and oil pump issues can occur without proper maintenance.
  • Maintenance Costs: Premium parts and high repair costs require a healthy budget.
  • Fuel Consumption: The V10 is thirsty, especially during spirited driving.

Bottom Line: The BMW M5 E60 is a modern classic, celebrated for its exceptional V10 engine and pure driving pleasure. However, ownership demands diligent maintenance and a serious budget.
